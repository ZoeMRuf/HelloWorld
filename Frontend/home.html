About the Project

Why?

A Geography student and a Computer-Science student come into a bar...

... all two-dimensional Maps of the World must deal with the problem of "projection". Due to the three-dimensional nature of the earth, it is not possible to reproduce a world map exactly in two dimensions.

While this is true of any map, these distortions reach extremes in a world map. Many techniques have been developed to create world maps that address diverse technical and aesthetic goals.

Learn More ...

What?

The Information that can be displayed on a Map is huge!

Political maps emphasize territorial boundaries and human settlement. Physical maps show geographical features such as mountains, soil type, or land use. Geological maps show not only the surface, but characteristics of the underlying rock, fault lines, and subsurface structures. Choropleth maps use color hue and intensity to contrast differences between regions, such as demographic or economic statistics.

And much more ...

With our Project we want to create awareness to the fact that the traditional Maps we all know are incorrect in many important points. Also, we want to create an interactive World Map where the user can choose the information mose relevant for them to show on the Map.

How?

... to use this Website.

First: Watch the video to get a basic understanding of the problem.

Second: Have a look at the "The True Size of ..." Project, to see how the size of a country changes depending on where it is located on the map.

Third: Log in to your Account or create one to use all the features of our Maps.

Fourth: You can now explore the different maps, all suited for one special use case. Have Fun!
